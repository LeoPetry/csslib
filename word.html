How to improve your site loading time:

Because of mobile, loading times are again a major concern for a web developer. Here at Fiddlefly we strive to deliver fast websites to our clients. How we do it? Well, there are a series of steps to fully optimize your site, but you should definitely start by making sure your images and graphics are correctly compressed and optimized.

Usually images are the biggest files - excluding other types of media - in a website. And, they often outnumber other kinds of files. This means that most of the time that it takes to display a complete webpage is actually spent requesting and downloading images.

Let's get to it:

To save an image for the web you can use the finger-twisting shortcut: cmd + alt + shift + s (Photoshop and Illustrator). There are 3 main types of image extensions:

.jpg

Jpegs are the most common format and offer the best compression for photographs. However, they do not support transparency and won't render a graphic perfectly.
